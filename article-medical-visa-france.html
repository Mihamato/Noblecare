Medical Visa for France:
The Complete Guide for African Patients

6 min read  ·  France  ·  Updated January 2025

Obtaining a French medical visa is one of the most decisive — and misunderstood — steps in planning medical travel to Paris. This guide explains the exact requirements, common pitfalls, and how to maximise your chances of approval on the first application.

What Is a French Medical Visa?

A French medical visa (visa pour soins médicaux) is a long-stay Schengen visa that allows foreign nationals to enter France specifically to receive medical treatment. It differs from a standard tourist visa in that it requires documented medical justification and is typically granted for the duration of the planned treatment — which can range from a few weeks to several months.

Patients from Nigeria, Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Mali, Cameroon, Gabon, and other French-speaking and English-speaking African countries apply through their nearest French consulate or visa application centre (TLS Contact or VFS Global).

"A well-prepared medical visa application is rarely refused. The key is knowing exactly what documentation is required — and presenting it in the right format."

Required Documents: The Complete List

Standard Documents Required by Every Consulate

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
  • Completed long-stay visa application form (CERFA)
  • Recent passport photographs (biometric format)
  • Proof of accommodation in France (hotel booking or hospital letter)
  • Return flight reservation (not necessarily purchased)
  • Travel insurance covering medical repatriation (minimum €30,000 coverage)
  • Proof of financial means (bank statements, last 3 months)
  • Letter from a French hospital confirming an appointment
  • Letter from your treating physician in your home country
  • Full medical file (diagnosis, test results, imaging)
  • Proof of payment or financial guarantee to the hospital

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Obtain your hospital appointment letter first. The French consulate requires written confirmation from a French hospital or clinic that they will treat you. This is the most critical document in the file.
  2. Prepare your medical file in French or English. Ensure all documents — including reports, scans, and lab results — are translated if necessary. Official translations are strongly recommended.
  3. Book your visa appointment at the TLS Contact or French consulate in your country. Wait times vary by location — in Lagos and Abidjan, appointments can be booked 2–4 weeks in advance.
  4. Submit your complete file at the visa appointment. Bring all originals and copies. Incomplete files are the primary cause of delay or refusal.
  5. Wait for processing. Standard processing takes 15 calendar days. Priority processing (available in some countries) takes 5 days. Complex cases may take up to 30 days.
  6. Collect your visa or be notified of additional requirements. If your visa is refused, you have the right to appeal within 2 months.

Most common reasons for refusal: Missing hospital confirmation letter · Insufficient proof of financial means · No confirmed return travel · Medical file not translated · Incomplete application form. All of these are avoidable with careful preparation.

How Long Does It Take?

From the moment you have your hospital appointment confirmed, allow approximately 3 to 4 weeks to obtain your French medical visa through standard processing. With priority processing and a complete file, this can be reduced to 10 days.

Special Considerations for Urgent Cases

If your medical situation is urgent — for instance, a rapidly progressing cancer requiring immediate treatment — the French consulate can process medical visa applications on an expedited basis. This requires a letter from the French hospital explicitly stating the urgency of the case and a recommended treatment start date.

In genuine emergencies, visas have been issued within 48 to 72 hours. NobleCare has experience managing urgent cases and knows how to present your file to maximise the likelihood of rapid approval.
